§ 01 — Problem Statement

Why This Invention Exists

Gardens and public parks require continuous maintenance — but every existing solution fails in a critical way.

PROB-01

Human Labour is Inconsistent

Manual dust and debris cleaning is slow, expensive, requires many workers, and gives no guarantee of completeness.

PROB-02

Ground Robots Can't Avoid Flowers

Traditional lawn robots cannot distinguish flowers from grass — they damage or destroy garden plants.

PROB-03

No Single Robot Does All Tasks

No existing robot can clean, cut grass, clean water bodies, and irrigate — all autonomously, as one system.

PROB-04

No Commercial Aerial Solution

There is no patented or commercial aerial drone product that performs all park maintenance tasks autonomously under a single platform.

§ 03 — Nightly Autonomous Cycle

From 1 AM to 7 AM

A precise, sequenced operation that completes every maintenance task before visitors arrive — invisibly.

1:00 AM

Scout & Task Allocation

Scout drone scans all park zones, generates a zone map, and the main hub assigns tasks to nearest available drones using a greedy nearest-neighbour algorithm.

1:30 AM

Tool Retrieval at Sub-Hub

Drones fly to nearest sub-hub, and the tool carousel automatically attaches the required module — blade, arm, net, or basket — via dovetail + electromagnet + pogo pins.

2:00 AM

Dust Clearing

No tool module required. Drones hover at 20 cm above paths and use the onboard 40W brushless nozzle to blast fine dust away from walking surfaces.

2:30 AM

Leaf Collection

Swap to leaf basket module. Drones sweep paths collecting leaves and light debris, emptying the basket at sub-hub bins via servo-hinged lid.

3:00 AM

Grass Cutting

Swap to blade module. Drones hover at precisely 5 cm (±1 cm) above the lawn. YOLOv8 AI cuts only grass — flowers and plants are detected and skipped in real time at 15–20 FPS.

4:00 AM

Debris Collection

Swap to robotic arm module (2-DOF servo gripper, 200g payload). Drones pick up fallen branches and large debris, depositing them in sub-hub bins.

4:30 AM

Water Body Cleaning

Swap to net module. Drones hover over water bodies and deploy a 30 cm nano-mesh net downward to skim floating waste from the water surface.

5:00 AM

Irrigation

No tool required. Drones couple to in-ground drip system sockets via a retractable irrigation port — no onboard water tank needed. Water flows from the garden's existing drip network.

6:00 AM

Return & Recharge

All drones return to nearest sub-hub, swap battery in under 60 seconds, store tool module in carousel. Sub-hubs retract underground. Park opens at 7 AM with no visible infrastructure.

§ 04 — Interchangeable Tool Modules

One Drone. Four Missions.

The universal receiver bay (dovetail + electromagnet + pogo pins) accepts any one of four task-specific modules. One drone does every job.

⚙️

Blade Module

150mm rotary cutter disc, brushless motor, servo-retractable. Grass trimming at 5 cm hover altitude. AI skips flowers.

🦾

Robotic Arm Module

2-DOF servo gripper, 200g payload, 15cm reach. Picks fallen branches and large debris for transport to bin.

🕸️

Net Module

30cm nano-mesh net, servo-deployed downward. Skims floating waste from water body surfaces while hovering.

🧺

Leaf Basket Module

2L open collection basket, servo-hinged lid. Sweeps and collects leaves and light debris from paths.

§ 06 — Novelty vs. Prior Art

What No Existing Product Can Do

Every existing technology fails in at least one critical dimension. This invention solves all of them in a single system.

Robotic Lawn Mowers (Husqvarna, iRobot)

Ground-only; cannot avoid flowers; no water capability; no swarm
Aerial operation; AI multi-class plant detection; modular tools; water skimming from air

Agricultural Drone Sprayers (DJI Agras)

Single-function (spraying only); large size; no swarm protocol for gardens
Multi-function compact drone; park-scaled; full swarm coordination protocol

Drone-in-a-Box (Skydio, DJI)

Above-ground, visible; no waste handling; no tool swapping; single function
Underground retractable hubs + tool carousel + piped waste transport

FlyingToolbox (Westlake Univ. 2025)

Mid-air tool exchange requires two drones; no garden-specific tools defined
Hub-based ground swapping with one drone + hub; garden-specific modules

MIT Seaswarm Water Robots

Water-only; cannot transition to land; heavy; requires separate deployment
Single drone handles land tasks AND water surface cleaning via hovering net

Claim 1
An autonomous garden and park maintenance system comprising: (a) a main hub with central computer, solar array, and buffer battery; (b) a plurality of retractable sub-hubs with tool carousel, battery swap robot, waste bin, and landing pad; (c) a drone swarm each with universal tool receiver bay, AI vision, flight controller, and supercapacitor backup; (d) an underground waste pipe with regenerative blower; (e) a control system assigning tasks to the nearest drone based on a scout-generated zone map.
Claim 2
Interchangeable task-specific tool modules selected from: grass cutting blade module, robotic gripper arm module, nano-mesh water skimming net module, and leaf collection basket module.
Claim 3
Each sub-hub retracts into an underground vault via a scissor lift mechanism and is concealed by a flush lid during daytime — zero visual pollution.
Claim 4
A dual-bay landing pad with a first bay for tool swapping and a second bay for battery swapping, with a roller conveyor for moving a drone between bays.
Claim 5
A supercapacitor (50F, 16V) providing at least 10 seconds of emergency power on main battery failure, enabling controlled descent from operational altitude.
Claim 6
An AI vision system comprising OAK-D Pro W (RGB + depth + NIR) and FLIR Lepton 3.5 thermal sensor — enabling all-season, all-weather plant and obstacle classification.
Claim 7
A control system allocating tasks using a greedy nearest-neighbour algorithm based on Euclidean distance between each drone's location and a task cell centre — minimising energy per task.
Claim 8
A waste pipe with cleanout ports every 50 metres and a water jet nozzle powered by a pressure booster pump for automatic blockage clearing.
Claim 9
Each drone weighs less than 2 kg including any one tool module (1,985 g verified), qualifying as a micro drone under DGCA Drone Rules 2021.
Claim 10
An emergency fail-safe mode that disables a drone's radio upon detection of an unauthorised data access command and executes a pre-stored emergency landing routine — independent of hub communication.
